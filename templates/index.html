About Fairdressers

Why we need hairdressers (and/or barbers) to charge according to the haircut instead of the person who is getting it.

Forcing customers to declare a gender creates a difficult and often hostile situation for transgender and non-binary people.
Women who do not want a "ladies" hairstyle still pay three times the rate for a short haircut that is typically considered a "men's" style.
Charging extra for a "female" version of everyday products, often known as "The Pink Tax" exacerbates existing inequalities by making services more expensive for women.
